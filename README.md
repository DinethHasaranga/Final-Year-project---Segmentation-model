# Final-Year-Research-project---Segmentation-model
ANALYSIS OF BRAIN TUMOR SEGMENTATION FROM MRI IMAGES OF THE BRAIN USING UNET ARCHITECTURE WITH DIFFERENT ENCORDERS.  
Brain tumor segmentation from MRI images plays a critical role in early diagnosis and treatment planning. This research addresses the challenges associated with accurately segmenting brain tumors by developing and evaluating optimized UNet-based segmentation models with different encoders. The overarching aim is to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of brain tumor detection in MRI images. The methodology involves the development of UNet models incorporating various encoders, including VGG16 and Efficient Net. 

A comparative analysis is conducted using quantitative metrics such as sensitivity, recall, and Dice coefficient. The influence of encoder choice on segmentation performance is investigated across different tumor types and imaging conditions.

The main results of the research reveal significant variations in segmentation performance based on the choice of encoder architecture. While all models demonstrate promising segmentation accuracy, certain encoders exhibit superior performance in capturing subtle features of brain tumors.

In conclusion, this research contributes to the advancement of brain tumor segmentation through the development and evaluation of optimized UNet-based segmentation models. The findings underscore the importance of encoder choice in model performance and highlight the potential for improved diagnostic accuracy in clinical practice.
